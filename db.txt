The air in the cavern hung heavy and still, smelling of damp earth and unseen minerals. Elara gripped the hilt of her dagger, the cold steel a faint comfort against the gnawing fear. Ahead, the narrow passage opened into a vast, silent chamber. A single, enormous crystal pulsed with a sickly green light, casting long, jittering shadows that danced like trapped spirits. 'We must be cautious,' whispered Kael, his voice rough. 'The guardian of the tomb is said to sleep with one eye open.' She nodded, her boots crunching lightly on the powdered stone floor, and took the first brave step into the abyss.
